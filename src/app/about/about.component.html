CROSSING BORDERS
in the Insular Middle Ages

We need a front page for the site, explaining our principles of selection: material that has crossed linguistic and geographical materials, excluding originals and variants in original language that have travelled; noting that early print has not been included, and giving dates. Front page would also include bibliography, which details short form titles and abbreviations used. The bibliography will be populated by scholars cited (in short form) in the discursive entries. We also need to credit contributors on this page. The encyclopaedic entries for each text need to trace this history of movement, identifying notable stages of transmission/ translation. It should also make a claim for significance – why have we selected this text as a case study? These will be co-authored.
